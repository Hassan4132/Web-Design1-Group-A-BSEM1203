Understanding WAEC Marking Schemes

Learn how NPSE, BECE and WASSCE examination papers are marked and how to maximize your scores.



Many students focus only on answering questions correctly. However, understanding how examiners award marks can significantly improve examination performance. This guide explains how NPSE, BECE and WASSCE marking schemes work.

NPSE Marking Scheme

How NPSE Is Marked

NPSE objective questions are usually awarded one mark for each correct answer. Wrong answers do not receive marks. Students are encouraged to answer every question carefully.

Examiner Expectations

Read instructions carefully. Fill answer sheets properly. Avoid multiple answers for a single question. Manage time effectively.

BECE Marking Scheme

Objective Questions

Each correct answer attracts marks. Candidates should shade answers clearly and avoid erasures.

Theory Questions

Examiners award marks for correct steps, explanations and final answers. Showing calculations can earn partial marks.

Study Tip

Always show workings in Mathematics and Science subjects. Even if the final answer is incorrect, method marks may still be awarded.

WASSCE Marking Scheme

Objective Section

Multiple-choice questions are machine-marked. Each correct answer receives full marks.

Theory Section

Marks are awarded for: Correct definitions Accurate explanations Relevant points Proper calculations Correct conclusions

Essay Questions

Subjects such as English, Literature, Government and Economics require structured answers. Examiners look for organization, content quality and relevance.

NPSE vs BECE vs WASSCE


Exam Level Difficulty Marking Style
NPSE Primary Basic Mostly Objective
BECE Junior Secondary Moderate Objective + Theory
WASSCE Senior Secondary Advanced Objective + Theory + Essay

Tips From Examiners

Read Instructions

Many students lose marks because they fail to follow instructions correctly.

Show Your Work

Always show calculations and explanations where required.

Manage Time

Allocate time wisely across all sections of the examination.

Answer Clearly

Neat presentation improves readability and helps examiners award marks accurately.
